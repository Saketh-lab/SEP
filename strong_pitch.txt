We're building NeuralDB, a database that understands semantic meaning.

Problem: Traditional databases are dumb. They only match exact strings. If a customer asks "show me comfortable shoes for running," a normal database can't understand that "running shoes" and "athletic footwear" are related. E-commerce sites lose 40% of potential sales because search and recommendations fail.

Solution: We've built the first database with language models built in. Queries are semantic, not just keyword matching. Developers can say "find products similar to X" and our DB understands context, synonyms, and relationships without any manual tagging.

Technical Moat: We built this from scratch - not OpenAI wrappers. Our custom vector + graph hybrid architecture runs 100x faster than Pinecone or Weaviate on complex queries. We use quantization and custom CUDA kernels. All queries under 50ms at billion-record scale. We have 3 patents pending on our indexing approach.

Network Effects: Every query improves our semantic understanding. When Retailer A searches for "winter boots," Retailer B's similar products rank better. Shared embeddings across customers create a collective intelligence. More data = better understanding = more customers.
