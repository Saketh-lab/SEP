We're building NeuroLink OS, a brain-computer interface operating system.

Problem: BCIs (brain-computer interfaces) are coming. Neuralink, Synchron, and others are making hardware. But there's no software platform. It's like having smartphones without iOS or Android.

Solution: We're building the operating system for neural interfaces. Think: app store for your brain. Developers can build "neural apps" - direct thought-to-action interfaces. Control smart homes, type with thoughts, play games, browse the web - all through neural signals.

Technical Approach: We're hardware-agnostic. Our ML models decode neural signals from any BCI device. We've built:
- Universal signal processing layer
- Intent classification models (96% accuracy in lab tests)
- Developer SDK with Python/Swift/Kotlin support
- Privacy-first architecture (all processing on-device)

Current Stage: We're pre-revenue and pre-hardware. We've been developing on:
- EEG headsets (consumer-grade)
- Simulated neural data
- Partnership discussions with 2 BCI hardware companies

We haven't launched publicly yet. Our dev tools are in private alpha with 20 researchers.

Why Now: BCIs are 2-5 years from mainstream. Neuralink's human trials started. Synchron has FDA approval. Apple is rumored to be researching it. The hardware is coming - but the software layer doesn't exist. First mover advantage is massive.

Market: If BCIs reach 1% of smartphone penetration, that's 50M users. App ecosystem could be $100B+ (following mobile app economics).

Team:

Vision: Every human will eventually have a neural interface. We're building the software foundation for that future. When you think about calling your mom, your phone just dials. When you imagine a purple dragon, it renders in your AR glasses. Direct brain-to-computer communication.
